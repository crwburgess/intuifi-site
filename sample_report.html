🧠 Executive Summary
💡 TLDR
Across all ten accounts, persistent platform reliability failures in dashboards, integrations, and access are occurring simultaneously with unmet demand for deeper analytics, geographic expansion, and pricing transparency, creating compounding friction that spans both technical and commercial dimensions.
Strategic implication

The business is caught between the expectation of delivering advanced analytical value and the reality of unresolved foundational instability, making it difficult to credibly pursue expansion or renewal without first closing the reliability gap.

Main takeaways
✓

✓

✓

Tensions to be aware of
!

!
High signal volume, low resolution evidence
With 303 unclassified signals and repeated follow-up outcomes across named themes, the volume of open or unresolved interactions suggests that issue capture is outpacing issue closure across the account base.

Recommended next steps

1. Resolve core platform reliability now

Prioritise dashboard performance, Salesforce connector stability, SSO access, and claims analytics accuracy as a coordinated reliability sprint, since these issues are present across all accounts and are actively blocking both daily usage and renewal confidence.

2. Build a renewal risk register by account

Map each account's open technical issues against their renewal timeline so leadership can identify which clients carry compounded commercial and operational risk before the next renewal cycle closes.

3. Gate France expansion on performance benchmarks

Define and communicate clear dashboard speed and reliability thresholds that must be met before committing to the France roll-out, so that expansion interest is not converted into new accounts inheriting existing platform instability.

Roadmap risk

Active France roll-out interest and benchmarking requests create implicit roadmap commitments that are difficult to deliver credibly while foundational reliability issues consume engineering and support capacity across the existing account base.
